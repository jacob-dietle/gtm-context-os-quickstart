# Content Type Specifications

Output specs per content type. Each spec defines exactly what the document looks like so the agent knows what to produce before it starts writing.

---

## Newsletter Post

**Platform:** Substack / email newsletter
**Word count:** 800-1500 words
**Framework:** Insight-led

### Section Structure

| Section | Words | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 30-60 | One sentence that makes the reader stop. A claim, question, or observation. Not a summary. |
| Context bridge | 100-200 | Why this matters right now. Connect the hook to the reader's world. |
| Core argument | 300-500 | The main idea, developed with evidence. This is the meat. |
| Evidence/examples | 200-400 | Concrete examples, data, or experiences that support the argument. From corpus or author experience. |
| Practitioner takeaway | 100-200 | What the reader should do differently. Specific, actionable. |
| Closer | 30-60 | End with the strongest claim or the most interesting open question. No "In conclusion." |

### Rules
- Lead with the non-obvious insight, not the summary
- One core argument per post (if you have two, write two posts)
- Evidence from the author's actual work, not hypothetical
- No product pitch. Independent value test: would someone share this even if no product existed?

## Blog Post (Technical)

**Platform:** Personal site / Medium
**Word count:** 1500-3000 words
**Framework:** Problem-solution

### Section Structure

| Section | Words | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Problem framing | 200-400 | What's broken, and why people are experiencing it. Direct, specific. |
| Why it's hard | 200-400 | What makes this problem non-obvious. The thing most people miss. |
| Approach | 400-800 | How the author solved it. Step by step. Code or architecture if relevant. |
| Evidence | 300-600 | What happened when the approach was applied. Numbers, outcomes, specifics. |
| What to try | 200-400 | Practical next steps for the reader. Ordered by effort (easiest first). |

### Rules
- Technical depth matched to audience (practitioners, not beginners)
- Code examples are real, not sanitized/simplified to uselessness
- Admit what didn't work alongside what did
- No "best practices" framing (say "here's what worked for me")

## Blog Post (Opinion)

**Platform:** Personal site / Medium / LinkedIn article
**Word count:** 1000-2000 words
**Framework:** Thesis-driven

### Section Structure

| Section | Words | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Claim | 50-100 | State the thesis directly. One sentence if possible. |
| Supporting evidence | 400-600 | 2-3 pieces of evidence from experience, data, or observation. |
| Counterargument | 200-400 | Steel-man the other side. Then explain why you still hold your position. |
| Implications | 200-400 | What follows if the thesis is true. What should change. |
| Closer | 50-100 | The strongest restatement of the claim, or the question it opens. |

### Rules
- Take an actual position (not "it depends" unless genuinely ambiguous)
- The counterargument section is mandatory — it builds credibility
- Personal experience > third-party citations for opinion pieces
- Don't hedge the claim in the opening just to soften it later

## LinkedIn Post

**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Word count:** 150-300 words
**Framework:** PAS (compressed) or Hook-Develop-Land

### Section Structure

| Section | Words | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook (line 1-2) | 15-30 | Stop the scroll. Strong claim, surprising observation, or direct question. |
| Develop | 80-180 | 3-5 short paragraphs developing the point. One idea per paragraph. |
| Land | 30-60 | Takeaway or question. Invite response without being needy. |

### Rules
- First line must work in the preview (before "see more")
- One idea only. LinkedIn rewards clarity, not comprehensiveness.
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences). White space matters.
- No hashtag spam. 3-5 relevant hashtags max, at the end.
- No "I'm humbled/excited/thrilled to announce" — just say the thing.
- No product pitch unless it's genuinely the point (and even then, lead with value).

## Framework Selection Guide

| If the goal is... | Use this framework | Content types it fits |
|---|---|---|
| Share a non-obvious insight | Insight-led | Newsletter, LinkedIn |
| Explain how to solve a specific problem | Problem-solution | Blog (technical) |
| Argue a position | Thesis-driven | Blog (opinion), Newsletter |
| Describe a transformation | BAB (Before/After/Bridge) | Blog (opinion), Newsletter |
| Teach a specific process | Problem-solution | Blog (technical) |
| Provoke discussion | Thesis-driven or Hook-Develop-Land | LinkedIn, Newsletter |

When the author provides an outline, the outline structure takes precedence over these frameworks. The frameworks are defaults when no outline exists.
